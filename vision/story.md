# Linear Ascent — Story

Iconic-template fantasy (LOTR grammar: elves, dwarves, orcs, demons) **fused with futuristic tech** — arcanotech, not ancient. Same bones, but forges run on plasma, shields project fields, and your sidekick is a machine spirit. The story exists to explain the linear goal — the name of the tower is the name of the game.

## The premise

The world of **Aldervale** was whole once, and it was never primitive: humans ran the river ports and their signal towers, elves grew the bio-lit deep woods, dwarves split atoms in mountain fusion-forges. Magic and machine were one craft there — they called it **aether**.

Then **Vharuk, the Demon King** rose from below and did not burn the world — he **stole it**. Realm by realm, he tore the lands out of the ground and stacked them into a megastructure in the sky: a tower of a hundred floors, welded together with black iron, grav-engines, and chains of aether. The people call it **the Ascent**.

Every floor is a captured realm — a goblin-run meadowland patrolled by scrap-drones, a dwarven mine where the machines died screaming, an elven forest whose lights went dark. On every floor a **Warden** — half beast, half war-machine — holds the lift to the next. On the hundredth floor, in a citadel that is equal parts throne room and reactor core, sits Vharuk himself.

At the foot of the tower stands the last free settlement: **Roothollow**, a shantytown of refugees from every race, built from the wreckage of the realms — tarps over titanium, a plasma forge next to a horse trough. This is where every climber starts, and where every dead climber wakes up.

## The goal (one sentence)

**Climb the Ascent, free every floor, and cast down the Demon King on floor 100.**

The whole player base shares one number — the highest floor ever cleared. When a Warden falls, the lift opens *for everyone*, and the names of those who did it are cut into the **Stone of the Climb** in Roothollow's square — old granite, the names lit from within by aether.

## The races (flavor, light mechanics)

Playable characters are refugees of the stolen realms. Race is flavor plus a small nudge; class carries the mechanics (see [ideas.md](./ideas.md) §2):

- **Human** — adaptable: +1 energy cap. Port-town survivors; good with anything that has a trigger or a hilt.
- **Elf** — keen: +1 aether (mana) cap. Their bio-lit forest is floor 23; older elves refuse to say what walks there now.
- **Dwarf** — stubborn: +5% armor value. Their fusion-halls are floors 11–20; they want them back, room by room.
- **Halfling** — lucky: better rolls on presents and loot. Nobody remembers which floor was theirs, least of all the halflings.

## The enemies (tiered, familiar, tech-twisted)

The tower's garrison gets worse as you climb — a ladder players already know, with the Demon King's machinery bolted on:

wolves and **goblins with scrap-rifles** → **orc warbands in salvaged warframes** → the walking dead in rusted exo-rigs → spiders in the lightless server-deeps → fire wyrms nesting in reactor slag → frost giants → storm-things among wrecked sky-ships → shades that live in dead signals → **demons, flesh welded to engine** — and at the very top, the Demon King's own court.

## The sidekick in the story

Every climber is bonded to a **shardmind** — your Luna agent. In-world it is a machine spirit of old Aldervale, an aether intelligence living in a crystal shard that chose you at the tower gate. It remembers everything (that is what shards are for), scouts ahead through senses you don't have, drags you out of one death a day, and carries what you can't afford to lose. The shardminds' own quiet quest: their network only comes back online when the tower falls — your sidekick needs *you* to finish the climb.

## Why the story fits the mechanics

- **Stolen realms as floors** → each floor is a self-contained world (gate town, wilds, Warden) we can author one at a time.
- **The lift opens for everyone** → shared linear progression, community boss events.
- **Roothollow respawn** → death sends you home, not out of the game.
- **The Stone of the Climb** → permanent public credit (SAO's monument, LORD's daily news).
- **Arcanotech** → the item ladder can run from scrap-steel shivs to fusion-core blades without breaking theme, and the terminal-style text UI is diegetic — you are literally reading a shardmind's feed.
- **The name** — *Linear Ascent* is literally the object of the game: the Ascent, climbed in a line, floor by floor.
