# Linear Ascent — Vision

*Name: **Linear Ascent** (renamed from the initial working title "Sidequest" on 2026-07-23 — the name states the goal: the tower is the Ascent, climbed floor by floor).*
*Captured 2026-07-23 from Roy's original brief. Companions: [story.md](./story.md) · [world.md](./world.md) · [economy.md](./economy.md) · [ideas.md](./ideas.md) · [research.md](./research.md)*

**Setting update (2026-07-23):** not purely ancient LOTR — the world is **arcanotech**: the same iconic races and tower, tech-enhanced (plasma forges, warframes, energy shields, the sidekick as a machine spirit). Futuristic tech layered on the same structure. See [story.md](./story.md).

## The core idea

A text game like the old BBS door games — DND-style, everything multiple choice. You pick a character presented in text, and you move through situations by choosing from options:

- **In the village** you can go to: the blacksmith, the potions shop, the lodge, the bank, the pawn shop.
- **Leave the village** and go to: the forest, the caves, the mountains.
- **Encounters** are text situations with choices. You encounter a dwarf — they ask you for X. You find a dragon with 6/7 attack/defense — what do you do: *stand, attack, run*?
- **Combat is consequential.** You attack the dragon, lose one defense, it attacks back — you lose your 6 energy points and die. You lose all your armour and money. You respawn in the village.

## Multiplayer and offline vulnerability

- The game is multiplayer among people playing the game.
- If you don't sleep in the lodge, other players can kill you while you're not there.
- When you come back after a day away, you get presents — with an element of luck, so the reward is variable.
- We will likely need a database (hosted on Render) to save multiplayer data.

## The bank

- The bank saves your money and pays interest.
- Money in the bank survives death — it's like a lodge for your money.

## The agent as sidekick

It's not just you playing — it's **you and your agent** as a sidekick in the game. (Ideas developed in [ideas.md](./ideas.md).)

## Group play

Creative multiplayer directions:

- You can build something others can attack, or join in.
- Players form groups to fight other groups.
- Specific towers or fortresses that need multiple people to attack.
- Possibly WoW-style character abilities that combine: a tank type with more strength, a sorcerer, a paladin, an arrow fighter — though that may not fit this interface model. To be evaluated.

## Pacing: energy as the retention engine

The objective: players run out of energy/mana and **have to wait**. The game deliberately takes time and makes people come back:

- Avoid players killing hours on it in one sitting.
- The return happens when mana/energy regenerates — forced breaks.
- You can obviously buy/earn energy increases.

## Naming

Many great games carry their main purpose or goal in their name. Sword Art Online showed its structure in levels — each level a whole new world, on a flying island — linear in its progression explanation.

- Initial working name was **Sidequest**; the final name should portray our **linear progression goal**, and the story of the game should support that.
- **Decided 2026-07-23: the name is Linear Ascent.** In-world, the tower itself is called "the Ascent" ([story.md](./story.md)), so the name is both the goal and the place.
